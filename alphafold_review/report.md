In this Nature paper, the authors are trying to address the slow and expensive process of identifying
the 3-D structures of protein sequences. Only about 100,000 of these structures have been solved experimentally. 
Using modern technology and programming techniques, the authors attempt to address this major challenge in biology.

The authors use very advanced machine learning strategies in this paper. For instance, neural networks were 
used to create a deep learning model that understands spatial relationships between residues and the physical 
constraints of proteins. This model uses a recycling method to predict protein structures and feeds that knowledge 
back into the network. The model also uses an attention-based design that models relationships between amino acid 
positions to help improve its learning.
